Rubric for discussion posts

A

A-level discussion posts are well above average:

  • answer the prompt in a manner resplendent with individual insights and ideas that connect the words in the text (quoted and paraphrased) to examples the outside world, past experience, or ways of thinking.
  • The writer attempts to go beyond simply answering the prompt and stimulates further thought and discussion.
  • The response will be more than a paragraph, logically constructed, and clearly written.
B

B-level discussion posts are above average:

  • answer the prompt, connecting the words in the text (quoted and paraphrased) and providing outside connections or examples,
  • but do not go much beyond the prompt or add anything new to the discussion.
  • The response will be more than a paragraph, logically constructed, and clearly written.
C

C-level discussion posts are average:

  • answer all questions asked with obvious information and minimal quotations,
  • but do not provide outside connections or examples.
  • The response may be more than a paragraph but lack a depth of knowledge.
D

D-level discussion posts are marked more by what they lack rather than what they have, and will be deficient one or more of the following areas:

  • will not accurately address the prompt,
  • will fail to provide quotations,
  • will fail to be a multi-paragraph response,
  • and/or will be sloppy and hard for readers to understand.
Note on responses:

In your replies to other students' work, you will receive full credit for comments that make a clear, honest effort to engage with the original poster, responding to their writing in a way that goes beyond the obvious.

Simply agreeing or disagreeing with other class members is not sufficient, and will count as failure to reply. Failing to reply to other students' discussion posts will result in a 20% deduction.
