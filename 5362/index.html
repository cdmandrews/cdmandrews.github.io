Catalog Description

Studies in the theory and practice of electronic research, writing and editing. Includes topics that relate to academic scholarship and contemporary writing instruction within electronic settings.

About the Course

Circulation. Distribution. Delivery. Collaboration. The fun, messy stuff that goes along with digital writing AFTER you hit the “publish” button. How does discourse circulate to achieve goals? Whose goals are met when discourse is put into motion? What happens when discourse is on the go?

This course overviews digital rhetoric: an interdisciplinary field offering scholars, teachers, and makers an array of theories, methods, and practices for working with digital texts. We will survey scholarship in digital rhetoric and explore the relevance of that work for your pedagogical, professional, and personal writing practices. You will practice digital research methods to develop a research project on a topic of your choosing. You will also add to your technical and rhetorical skills to evaluate and compose accessible and usable digital texts in a variety of media and on topics that you’ll develop expertise in.

A spirit of tinkering and experimentation around both what we read and what we build will be at the heart of everything we’ll do. No particular technical expertise is prerequisite for the course.

Course Outcomes

In this course you will:

  1. Develop skills in composing with attention to multiple and layered elements of digital writing conventions as you create rhetorical, digital, multimodal texts
  2. Develop perspectives and strategies for incorporating digital multimodal composition into your pedagogical and/or professional practices
  3. Advance your proficiency in scholarly methods of research and inquiry
