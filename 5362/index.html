Catalog Description

Studies in the theory and practice of electronic research, writing and editing. Includes topics that relate to academic scholarship and contemporary writing instruction within electronic settings.

About the Course

Webtexts, broadly defined, are texts “authored specifically for publication on the World Wide Web”: they are hypertextual and multimodal. They wed rhetoric, design, and code to enact arguments and perform meaning.

In this course, we’ll mix survey and workshop to explore both the whys and the hows of scholarly webtexts and digital publishing. We will read broadly in digital rhetoric scholarship and explore the relevance of that work for our pedagogical, professional, and personal practices. We will also add to your technical and rhetorical practices as we create accessible, rhetorical digital webtexts in a variety of media and on topics that you’ll develop expertise in.

A spirit of tinkering and experimentation around both what we read and what we build will be at the heart of everything we’ll do. No particular technical expertise is prerequisite for the course: Beginners Welcome!

Course Outcomes

In this course you will:

  1. Develop skills in composing with attention to multiple and layered elements of digital writing conventions as you create rhetorical, digital, multimodal texts
  2. Develop perspectives and strategies for incorporating digital multimodal composition into your pedagogical and/or professional practices
  3. Advance your proficiency in scholarly methods of research and inquiry
