In case you were feeling nostalgic.
An occasional poem.

In case you were feeling nostalgic
about dinnertime
with your children.

In particular,

In case you were feeling cats-in-the-cradle
about missed time
with your loving children,

let me tell you a story.

4:45 pm

Sidney and Haley come over
from next door
with Flamin Hot (TM) Cheetos.
Your neverending hungerbucket
just had to have some.

When rebuffed, he came trolling
the pantry
for snacks.

Frieda sees me chopping calabaza
and declares she will
NOT
eat
potatoes.

5:00 pm

I chase the neverending hungerbucket
O.
U.
T.
of the pantry
for snacks.

He screams with firey face,
stork's bite aglow
with the intensity of his age.

I tell him to go upstairs.
NOOOOOOOO

Five.

Four.

Three.

Two.

One and a third.

One and one half.
(Have I lost the ability to count so soon in the summer?)

One and one quarter.

One.

NOOOOOOOOOOO

I lift the two ton
squirming
neverending hungerbucket
and deposit him
upon Transformer sheets
and instruct him to undergo a similar transformation.

5:15 pm

I WANNA PEANUT BUTTER AND JELLY SANDWICH.

You can have one at dinner time.

I WANNA PEANUT BUTTER AND JELLY SANDWICH NOW.

Asked and answered.

I WANNA--

ASKED AND ANSWERED.

YOU ARE MAKING ME SO MAD.

YOU ARE MAKING *ME* SO MAD.

Not even Cormac McCarthy could sort out this dialogue.

5:30 pm

I call Frieda and the neverending hungerbucket in to the kitchen.
with great gusto

Frieda, realizing the potatoes are baked
not roasted with sausage and carrots
not dripping in blood and ooze
not parboiled with brains
or cooked in a death sauce with pestilence
on the side,
happily orders a potato
with cheese
NO butter.

Adalaide,
who happily scrubbed
and poked
and wrapped
and ovened the potatoes with me
In an apalling, unexpected, and happy moment of consistency
happily orders a potato
with cheese
(lots)
NO butter.
But sure, bacon is okay, daddy.

All this time, the neverending hungerbucket
is collecting materials to construct
his PEANUT BUTTER AND JELLY SANDWICH NOW.

The PEANUT BUTTER is low, so he needs help.
I comply, swiftly
slapping it onto a slice of bread.
Then gently jiggling the jelly
out onto its mate.

5:45 pm

(or thereabouts)
I finally finish creating my masterwork baked potato.
A steaming bed of cotton-light Idaho glory
Dripping with (I don't think it's really) butter
A bit of salt
A bit more of pepper
C
H
E
E
E
S
E
A handful of grilled sliced onions, golden yellow and brown and
just slightly crispy on one side
Then to top it off, bacon bits.

Nay, not those maltodextrin-infused
soy powder bits,
injected with FD&C Red 40.

No, REAL Bacon.
Heated to glorious crispitude in cast iron.
Crumbled gently into tiny bits.
Refrigerated until just
this
moment.

It was beautiful.

5:51 pm

DADDY I WANT A POTATO NOW.

I sigh. Contemplating
the first bite
that I haven't yet taken.

Are you sure?

YES
says the hungerbucket.

Fine.
That potato is too hot to eat anyway
curls of steam rising sinfully up to heaven
like the taunting tail of a cigarette end.

I go into the kitchen,
expectancy and hunger inside me
and beside me.

I cut the potato in half.
I remove the devil skin.
I begin to chop the just-right soft potato.

DADDY I DONT WANT A POTATO I WANT
PEANUT BUTTER AND JELLY.

And with that, I go.
I sit down.
And I eat my potato.

6:08 pm

I am coming to the end. It was a wonderful time. My expectations for this rare baked potato night have been entirely fulfilled. I cooked them at the right temperature and for the right time, I got just enough (not too much) butter, made sure everyone had enough cheese, and the grilled onions really made the cold bacon pieces work.

And a happy accident: the calabaza squash I roasted (olive oil and sea salt; red pepper flakes on a small portion) and ate alongside the potato became a happy accident; the red pepper flakes and vegetable flavors popped right into the potato.

It was a glorious affair.

DADDY I WANT A PEANUT BUTTER AND--

CRASH.

The shatter of glass tears me away from the final two bites.
A pinprick of blood from a jar dropped onto saltillo tile must be dealt with.
And I contemplate the last nibbles of bacon and cheddary bliss
sweeping up the shards of my evening
and wondering if I'll get all the jelly up
or if the ants will get to have theirs tonight, too.
