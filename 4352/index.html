Accessibility and Course Delivery

I work continually to ensure this course’s design, delivery, and policies are as radically accessible as I can make them. I am here to help you succeed. If you have questions or specific accessibility needs that are not being met, I welcome dialogue with you.

Catalog Description

This course is the culminating experience for the Writing Studies emphasis in English. The course invites you to review, reflect on, integrate, and apply your learning from previous courses and experiences. You will create digital portfolios for career and publishing opportunities, emphasizing selection, revision, reflection, and presentation. In addition, you will research current issues facing writing programs and English departments and create a recommendation report necessary curricular or pedagogical revisions for the English department and Writing Studies track.

English B.A. Student Learning Outcomes

At the end of the program, students will be able to:

  1. Analyze the characteristics of language in terms of literary or discourse genres, conventions, and linguistic features.
  2. Apply effective and appropriate literary/rhetorical/linguistic methods and strategies in writing and/or in analyzing texts (spoken, written, and multimodal).
  3. Demonstrate understanding of literary/rhetorical traditions, as well as the historical and cultural contexts important to those traditions.
  4. Demonstrate foundational understanding of language systems, language development, and language in use.
  5. Apply and/or integrate appropriate critical terms and theoretical concepts and perspectives in writing and analyzing texts.
  6. Demonstrate ability to choose and use appropriate strategies to produce texts in a variety of genres to most effectively achieve specific purposes with specific audiences.
  7. Demonstrate the ability to locate, select, assess, and analyze information sources, both print and digital, and to integrate and document appropriately those sources in their own work.
  8. Demonstrate understanding of community and social relations, diverse, multicultural histories; and what it means to live in a global society.

ENGL 4352 Student Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Synthesize learning from previous courses and learning experiences.
  • Apply the principles informing writing studies (see appendices: "threshold concepts," "kinds of knowledge about writing," and "habits of mind") to creative, technical, pedagogical, and/or civic writing projects or situations that call for writing.
  • Apply appropriate research and information literacy practices to technical, creative, pedagogical, and/or civic writing projects or situations that call for writing.
  • Write in a variety of genres and media to achieve specific purposes in specific rhetorical situations.
  • Select and use strategic writing processes (invention, planning, drafting, revising, editing, repurposing) for specific rhetorical situations..
  • Demonstrate linguistic, rhetorical, genre, and editorial awareness and competence with language choices.

Preparation and Process

20% of final grade (Each week)

  • Every memo, draft, revision, editing, and final submission of in-class and homework due for the portfolios will count in this category. If you are absent on a day when a draft is due but post your draft on the BB discussion forum before the deadline, you will receive credit for the draft. Credit for reading/responding to drafts online (if absent) may be made up, see note below re: late work/extensions.
  • Reading Journal—For each reading, provide three quotes along with a minimum 50-word response for each. (10 entries)
  • Reflecting, Re-visioning, Reflecting Activity Memo—Four times this semester, you will choose an assignment you have done in a previous course, reflect on what you did, how it contributed to your learning, what you could do to revise it, re-seeing it in a different way. You will consider how the experience might contribute to future learning and what kinds of learning / knowledge you may be able to "transfer" forward.
  • Learning and Engagement Memo—Four times this semester, you will reflect on how you have engaged with the course work during the preceding week(s) and consider how your learning has expanded, as you consider our current course and previous courses and learning experiences. In these reflections, you will use our course outcomes to frame and focus your reflections. These overviews will refer to specific evidence to support your reflections.

Strategic Portfolios 1 & 2

Portfolio 1: Job Search/Grad School Materials: 20% of final grade | Full description

Portfolio 2: Capstone Portfolio; 40% of final grade | Full description

Throughout the semester, you will work on developing / assembling two "Strategic Portfolios." By "strategic" we will mean that these portfolios are meant for a specific purpose, individual, organization, or goal; P1 will be a collection of materials appropriate for a professional job application; P2 will be a capstone portfolio.

You will complete the following steps in this development process, documenting your work for each step. By the end of the semester, you will have two portfolios that represent you as a learner and a writer, highlighting your skills by presenting examples of work you have done and by presenting thoughtful analyses / reflections on your skills, knowledge, and experience as represented by the work you share.

  • explore, select presentation platform / software tool(s)
  • develop frameworks for and determine usability of design choices
  • develop frameworks that most effectively demonstrate your overall integration and synthesis of learning in your Writing Studies work
  • select evidence, using frameworks to evaluate and choose
  • share & respond, revise, edit selected evidence
  • draft, share & respond, revise, edit reflective overviews
  • Portfolio Presentations: When each portfolio is due, you will briefly present it to the class and explain the choices you made, how these reflect and synthesize your learning and engagement from this course and previous courses and experiences.

Revised Research Project

20% of final grade | Full description

You will write a polished, well-researched article, conference presentation, or other research-based publication, based on a previously written project. In class discussions and assignments, we will take a particular focus on research methods for writing studies, and part of your task will be to revisit and possibly expand your research methods for that project. To support your work, we will work from Try This: Research Methods for Writers, an open-access text from the WAC Clearinghouse (https://wac.colostate.edu/books/practice/try/). This project will be an extended process with several steps.

  • Research Plan, including targeted journal or conference, audience, and intended changes
  • Consultation: Notes from a consultation with appropriate expert in the field
  • Reflection on and articulation of appropriate research methods
  • Serious, global revision
  • Close careful editing
